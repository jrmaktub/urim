## PART 2: GOVERNMENT GRANTS (BIG MONEY, LONGER TIMELINE)

### A. Department of Energy — Critical Minerals Programs

The DOE is the single largest grant source in this space.

**1. $500M Battery Materials Processing & Manufacturing NOFO**
- **Status:** Announced but NOT yet published. This is the one to watch.
- **Action:** Monitor [energy.gov/cmm](https://www.energy.gov/cmm/critical-minerals-and-materials-program) and [grants.gov](https://www.grants.gov) daily.
- **Angle:** Position URIM as market infrastructure/price transparency layer for battery material supply chains.

**2. $50M Critical Minerals and Materials Accelerator**
- NOI issued August 2025, full NOFO expected 2026.
- Ideal fit for URIM's price discovery tech.

**3. Past DOE awards (closed but recurring):**
- $134M for REE supply chain R&D
- $355M for mines & metals capacity
- $40M ARPA-E RECOVER program
- These programs cycle. New NOFOs will come.

### B. Defense Production Act (DPA) — The Biggest Pipe

The U.S. government is throwing **billions** at critical minerals via DPA:

- **$2B** for FY2025 National Defense Stockpile purchases
- **$5B** Industrial Base Fund for critical minerals supply chains
- **$1B** DPA financing through September 2027
- **$500M** Office of Strategic Capital for loans and grants

**Antimony-specific DPA awards already made:**
- Perpetua Resources: $84.8M grant + $245M antimony offtake
- Nova Minerals: $43.4M for antimony mining in Alaska
- US Antimony Corp: $44M DOE funding request (Jan 2026)

**URIM's angle:** Position as the **price transparency and trading infrastructure** layer that these funded producers and DOD procurement offices need. Submit white papers to DLA Broad Agency Announcements.

### C. NSF SBIR/STTR

- **Phase I:** Up to ~$314,000 (proof of concept)
- **Phase II:** Up to ~$2.1M (development)
- **Status:** SBIR authority expired Sept 30, 2025. Reauthorization (H.R. 5100) in progress.
- **Action NOW:** Register on [SAM.gov](https://sam.gov) (takes weeks to process). Be ready to submit the moment it reopens.
- **Angle:** Novel application of blockchain technology for strategic commodity price discovery.

### D. EU Critical Raw Materials Act

If expanding to Europe is an option, this is massive:

- **€3B** RESourceEU Action Plan (adopted Dec 2025)
- **EIC Accelerator CRM Challenge:** €500K–€10M per project in blended finance
- **Innovation Fund 2026:** At least €700M for CRM and clean tech
- **European CRM Centre:** First matchmaking round **March 2026**

## PART 5: MONETIZATION STRATEGY

### Current Revenue Streams (Already Built)

| Stream | Rate | Status |
|--------|------|--------|
| Mineral Futures taker fee | 0.05% per position | Live on devnet |
| Prediction Market fee | 0.5% per bet | Live on devnet |
| Liquidation proceeds | 98% of liquidated collateral | Live on devnet |
| URIM token discount | 10% off fees for holders | Live on devnet |

### Near-Term Revenue Additions

**1. Market Data as a Service (B2B) — HIGH PRIORITY**
- Sell real-time critical mineral pricing data to institutional clients
- This is Polymarket's endgame (they got a $2B investment from ICE for this)
- URIM has unique on-chain price data for antimony/rare earths that doesn't exist elsewhere
- **Target customers:** Defense contractors, mining companies, commodity traders, hedge funds

**2. API Access / White-Label Licensing**
- License the mineral futures infrastructure to other DeFi protocols
- Charge for API access to on-chain price feeds
- Revenue: Monthly subscription or per-call pricing

**3. Premium Analytics Dashboard**
- Free: Basic price charts and positions
- Paid ($29-99/mo): Historical analysis, supply chain intelligence, trade signals, liquidation alerts
- Target: Retail traders and small commodity firms

**4. Subscription Tiers**
- **Free:** Basic trading access
- **Pro ($49/mo):** Advanced charting, alert system, reduced fees
- **Institutional ($499/mo):** API access, bulk data, custom integrations, OTC desk

### Medium-Term Revenue

**5. Institutional OTC Desk**
- Offer over-the-counter trades for large positions
- Charge spread + advisory fee
- Target: Mining companies hedging production, defense procurement offices

**6. Commodity Index Products**
- Create a "Critical Minerals Index" token tracking weighted basket of antimony, lithium, cobalt, copper
- Charge management fee (0.5-2% annually)
- List on DEXes for passive exposure to critical minerals

**7. Cross-Chain Expansion**
- Deploy on Ethereum L2s (Base, Arbitrum) for larger institutional audience
- Each chain = new fee revenue stream

### Monetization Lessons from Comparable Platforms

| Platform | Model | Volume | Revenue |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|
| **Kalshi** | 1.2% per trade | $50B annualized | Revenue-positive |
| **Polymarket** | 0% fees → data licensing | $23.5B volume | $0 protocol revenue (strategic) |
| **dYdX** | 0.02-0.05% maker/taker | $500B+ cumulative | Fee revenue + token |
| **GMX** | 0.1% open/close | $200B+ cumulative | Fee sharing with LPs |

**Recommended approach:** Start with current low fees (0.05%) to build liquidity, then layer on B2B data products and subscription tiers as volume grows.
